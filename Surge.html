Surge Adventure Park

Website: https://surgefun.com/locations/richmond/

Surge Adventure Park is an indoor trampoline park and arcade in Regency Square Mall, and is my overall pick for most fun activity to do with a feral toddler. Foam pits, jungle gyms, games, and a massive elaborate labyrinth tower that will have you both exhausted by the time you leave.

Pros: 1. It's a blast for everyone. 2. Toddler-proof safety is a key feature that I love about the place. Everything is clean, well-padded, and enclosed, so no matter how much David falls flat on his face he never gets hurt (knock on wood).

Cons: 1. It does cost money. Expect to pay around $20 total for the two of you to play for 90 minutes. 2. It gets pretty hectic during rush hours especially once all the bigger kids show up, which is not conducive to toddler play, so it's ideal to go when older kids are in school or early in the morning when they first open.

Rating
  • Convenience/Accessibility: 4/5 Open 7 days a week and no reservations are required, but some planning is needed to avoid crowds..
  • Toddler-Proofing: 4.5/5 The place is safe but huge, so you'll want to keep a close eye on your little one to keep them from getting lost.
  • Repeatability: 5/5 Cost aside, this place is so ridiculously fun that we could spend virtually all day every day here.
  • Pro Tip: The arcade is awesome and full of flashing lights and things to press, so you can entertain your toddler without spending a dime on the games since they probably won't know the difference.
