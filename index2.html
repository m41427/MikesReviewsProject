Dad to the Bone

Entertaining a toddler is a grueling and thankless endeavor. They're insane and full of endless energy that wreaks havoc if not given a proper outlet. My son is hilarious, but as a work-from-home dad I'm with him 24/7, and am constantly on the hunt for new toddler-friendly activities around town since I can only take so much Ms. Rachel before I'm begging for the sweet release of death. For my reviews, I've limited activities to those geared towards climbing, running, and general excersion (i.e. playgrounds and parks, not zoos or aquariums) that'll all but guarantee a good night's sleep for everyone.

Rating System
  • Convenience/Accessibility: Does it require a reservation or can I just show up? Is it open 7 days a week or closed on weekends? 1 indicates a high level of getting your ducks in a row before going, whereas a 5 means you can just show up whenever you want and wing it.
  • Toddler-Proofing: 1 indicates an absolute death trap, like bringing your kid to an actual minefield on top of a building with no railings and covered in open power outlets. A 5 means you can set your toddler loose with virtually no risk of injury or destruction, like a padded cell lined with plushies. Everything in between just requires an appropriate degree of supervision and support.
  • Repeatability: This is a wildcard rating and is also subjective. A 1 might be fun, but it loses its novelty after one trip and your toddler will get tired of it quickly. A 5 is something you and your kid could do literally every day and never get tired of it.
